- The change narrows three public write boundaries from the incorrect shared permission to their
  exact source permissions and adds the matching Deputy Manager seed grant for deficiency return.
- Main risk: an incorrect permission mapping could expose or hide a state-changing completeness
  action. Mitigation: four permission-only actors each prove exactly one projected and invokable
  action; all cross-action calls prove standard denial and unchanged state/evidence counts.
- Object access, state/resource predicates, checklist join, deficiency history, reference/register
  semantics, canonical reload, and stale-write behavior were preserved and covered by the existing
  application API and frontend suites.
- No migration, dependency, protected-path, source-document, styling, money calculation, external
  communication, deployment, or git operation was introduced.
- Browser risk remains independently gated: local Chromium could not launch in the sandbox, so the
  orchestrator must pass the spec twice and collect all nine screenshots before commit.
