## 3. Legal and Policy Foundation

SFPCL provides financial assistance to its members under the Producer Company framework.

As per the SOP, Section 378ZK of the Companies Act, 2013 permits a Producer Company, subject to its Articles, to provide financial assistance to members by:

1. Granting credit facilities for a period not exceeding six months.
2. Providing secured loans or advances repayable within a period of three months to seven years.

The SOP states that SFPCL is following this section and is therefore eligible to provide financial assistance to its members.

The SOP is intended to ensure that every loan transaction is:

- Systematic.
- Aligned with internal control policies.
- Legally and regulatorily compliant.
- Supported by proper documentation.
- Approved through the defined authority matrix.
- Monitored for utilisation, recovery and closure.
- Auditable by internal and statutory auditors.

## 8. Stage 1: Initial Loan Request

### 8.1 Who Can Apply

A shareholder or member requiring a loan may initiate the process. The borrower can be:

- An individual farmer.
- A Farmer Producer Company.
- A shareholder / member eligible under SFPCL policy.

The SOP uses the following terms interchangeably depending on context:

- Borrower.
- Shareholder.
- Farmer.
- Loan applicant.
- Member.

### 8.2 Application Channel

The borrower may approach the company through the Credit Assessment Team, typically the Credit Manager, by submitting a loan inquiry through:

- Offline channel.
- Digital channel.
- Physical submission at the designated office.
- Electronic submission through the company’s loan application portal.

### 8.3 Loan Application Form

The borrower must submit the prescribed Loan Application Form.

The form must be signed by:

- Loan applicant.
- Nominee of the loan applicant.

The application must include:

1. Folio number of shares held in SFPCL.
2. Number of shares held in SFPCL.
3. Maximum permissible loan limit, calculated as per the per-share limit specified by the company.
4. Required loan amount.
5. Nominee details:
   - Name.
   - Age.
   - Aadhaar number.
   - PAN.
   - Gender.

### 8.4 KYC and Supporting Documents

The borrower must submit supporting KYC documents for both borrower and nominee.

The nominee must not be a minor.

Required documents include:

1. Self-attested PAN card copy.
2. Self-attested Aadhaar card copy.
3. Copy of share certificates.
4. Copy of land documents, specifically 7/12 extract.
5. Crop plan.
6. Recent bank statement for the past six months.

### 8.5 Application Completeness Check

The Deputy Manager – Finance verifies the application for completeness.

If complete:

- An acknowledgment is issued.
- A unique application reference number is generated.
- The reference number is entered in the Loan Request Register.
- The reference number is recorded on the original copy of the application form.
- The reference number is communicated to the borrower.

### 8.6 Application Numbering

The unique application number starts with:

```text
LO00000001
```

Further loans are numbered sequentially based on the Loan Register maintained by the Credit Manager in Excel.

### 8.7 Incomplete Applications

If the application is incomplete:

- The application is returned to the applicant.
- Deficiencies are listed.
- The borrower must rectify and resubmit.
- The Credit Manager may use Annexure L, the Rejection Note, to state deficiencies and communicate them to the borrower.

## 9. Stage 2: Credit Assessment Process

### 9.1 Loan Appraisal Note

After receiving and initially checking the loan application:

1. The Deputy Manager – Finance prepares the Loan Appraisal Note.
2. The note outlines loan details and eligibility.
3. The Credit Manager reviews the note.
4. The Loan Application Form and Loan Appraisal Note are submitted to the Sanction Committee.

### 9.2 TAT

The turnaround time for preparing the Loan Appraisal Note and submitting it to the Sanction Committee is:

**2 days from receipt of application**

This TAT applies to:

- Deputy Manager – Finance.
- Credit Manager.
- Credit Assessment Team.

### 9.3 Eligibility Criteria

The Credit Manager must assess and record the following in the Loan Appraisal Note:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Active membership | Applicant must be an active member of the FPC / company. |
| Default status | Applicant must not be in default for any FPC loan of SFPCL or its subsidiary or associate company. |
| Documentation | Applicant must submit land documents, KYC, recent bank statement and crop plan. |
| Acceptance of terms | Applicant must agree to loan sanction terms in the Term Sheet and Loan Agreement. |
| Purpose | Loan purpose must relate to crop production and agricultural activity only. |

### 9.4 Rejection at Credit Assessment Stage

If the borrower fails to meet any required condition:

- The Credit Manager rejects the loan application.
- The reason for rejection is communicated through a Rejection Note.
- Communication may be sent by email or courier.
- The borrower may reapply after fulfilling the rejected criteria.

## 10. Loan Limit Determination

The final eligible loan amount is restricted to the lower of two limits:

1. Shareholding-based limit.
2. Agricultural land-based limit.

### 10.1 Shareholding-Based Limit

Formula:

```text
Loan Limit = Number of shares held × 30% of valuation per share
```

Loan entitlement is linked to the borrower’s equity holding in SFPCL.

### 10.2 Agricultural Land-Based Limit

Formula:

```text
Loan Limit = Per-acre cost of cultivation fixed annually by the company × Farmer’s land area under cultivation
```

This is based on the Scale of Finance, representing per-acre cost of cultivation.

For the current period, the Scale of Finance is capped at:

```text
₹20,000 per acre
```

### 10.3 Final Eligible Amount

```text
Final Eligible Loan Amount = Lower of Shareholding-Based Limit and Agricultural Land-Based Limit
```

### 10.4 Share Valuation

Share valuation is determined as follows:

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Basis | Latest audited financial statements approved at AGM |
| Method | Net Asset Value Method based on Fair Market Valuation |
| Frequency | Yearly |
| Timing | After statutory audit and before adoption of financial statements at AGM |
| Applicability | Used as base for loan limit eligibility |

### 10.5 Percentage Ambiguity Requiring Client Confirmation

The SOP contains an important inconsistency:

- The loan limit formula states: **Number of shares × 30% of valuation per share**.
- The share valuation section states that the base is **10% of share value**.
- The SOP also states that the current result of 10% valuation is **₹200 per share**.

This must be resolved before system configuration or operational rollout.

Recommended client decision required:

```text
Confirm whether the operative loan limit percentage is 30% of valuation per share, 10% of valuation per share, or ₹200 per share as the currently approved cap.
```

### 10.6 Board Approval for Percentage Change

Management may change the applicable percentage depending on SFPCL’s financial situation. Any change must be approved in a Board Meeting.

## 11. Active and Inactive Member Status

The meaning of “Active Member” is based on SFPCL’s Articles of Association.

### 11.1 Individual Member Eligibility

An individual member must:

1. Avail, directly or indirectly, services offered by the company during the period of membership. Examples:
   - Crop production.
   - Procurement.
   - Purchases.
   - Sale of agricultural inputs.

2. Supply primary produce produced by the member for a continuous period of four financial years, as on the last date of the previous financial year, to any of the following:
   - SFPCL.
   - Subsidiaries of SFPCL.
   - Step-down subsidiaries of SFPCL.
   - Through a Producer Institution member in which the individual is also a member and which supplies to SFPCL or its subsidiaries / step-down subsidiaries.

### 11.2 Relaxation for New / Recent Individual Members

The four-year condition does not apply if the member has supplied produce for at least one year to:

- SFPCL.
- Subsidiaries of SFPCL.
- Step-down subsidiaries of SFPCL.
- Through a Producer Institution member that supplies to SFPCL or its subsidiaries / step-down subsidiaries.

Alternatively, a Producer Member qualifies if they have provided services in employment or another capacity for a continuous period of three years to:

- SFPCL.
- Subsidiaries of SFPCL.
- Step-down subsidiaries of SFPCL.

### 11.3 Producer Institution Eligibility

A Producer Company or institutional member must:

1. Be a member of SFPCL.
2. Avail, directly or indirectly, services offered by SFPCL during the period of membership.
3. Supply primary produce for a continuous period of four financial years, as on the last date of the previous financial year, to:
   - SFPCL.
   - Subsidiaries of SFPCL.
   - Step-down subsidiaries of SFPCL.

### 11.4 Relaxation for New / Recent Producer Institutions

The four-year condition does not apply to member producer companies that have supplied produce for at least one year to:

- SFPCL.
- Subsidiaries of SFPCL.
- Step-down subsidiaries of SFPCL.

## 12. Stage 3: Credit Scrutiny and Approval

### 12.1 Sanction Committee Review

After receiving the Loan Application Form and Loan Appraisal Note, the Sanction Committee conducts detailed scrutiny.

The checks include:

1. **Eligibility verification**  
   Confirm borrower meets membership, shareholding and other eligibility criteria.

2. **Loan amount assessment**  
   Verify the loan amount is within permissible limits based on shareholding and company rules.

3. **Purpose of loan**  
   Ensure loan purpose aligns with company objectives and approved lending policy.

4. **Compliance checks**  
   Confirm adherence to internal policies, Companies Act, Producer Company provisions and regulatory guidelines.

5. **Past borrowing history**  
   Review previous loans, repayment discipline and defaults.

6. **Risk assessment**  
   Consider market, operational and borrower-specific risks, along with mitigation measures.

7. **Documentation completeness**  
   Ensure required documents are submitted and authenticated.

### 12.2 Credit Sanction Register

The Sanction Committee must record:

- Approval or rejection decision.
- Reasons for approval or rejection.
- Applicable approval authority.
- Any required abstentions.
- Exception approvals, where applicable.

The decision is recorded in the Credit Sanction Register and communicated by email to the Credit Assessment Team.

### 12.3 Approval Matrix

| Decision | Limit | Authority | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Loan sanction | Up to ₹5,00,000 per member | CFO + one Director from Sanction Committee | Maker-checker mechanism. Credit Manager is maker and Sanction Committee member is checker. |
| Loan sanction | Above ₹5,00,000 per member | CFO + two Directors from Sanction Committee | Joint approval required in Credit Sanction Register. |
| Loan sanction | Exceeding maximum permissible limit applicable per member | CFO + two Directors from Sanction Committee | Reason must be recorded in Exception Register with joint approval. |
| Disbursement process initiation | Not amount-specific | Senior Manager – Finance | Must verify documentation and SAP posting before release. |
| Execution of security documents | Not amount-specific | Company Secretary | Acts under PoA for stamping and handling SH-4 and blank-dated cheques. |

### 12.4 Special Cases: Director, Sanction Committee Member or Relative as Borrower

If any Sanction Committee member, director or relative is the borrower:

1. The loan must be scrutinised and approved only by the remaining Sanction Committee members, excluding the applicant.
2. The loan can be granted only with approval of members in a general meeting in accordance with Section 378ZK of the Companies Act, 2013.

### 12.5 Rejection by Sanction Committee

If the Sanction Committee rejects the loan:

- The Credit Assessment Team informs the borrower.
- The reason is based on the Sanction Committee’s reasoning.
- The borrower can reapply in the future after fulfilling the rejection criteria.

## 13. Stage 4: Documentation and Stamping

Stage 4 begins after Sanction Committee approval.

### 13.1 Documents Collected from Borrower

The Compliance Team collects and verifies:

1. PAN and Aadhaar copy of witness.
2. Cancelled cheque of applicant’s bank account.
3. One blank-dated cheque as security.

The witness must be an existing shareholder of SFPCL.

### 13.2 Cancelled Cheque

The cancelled cheque is used to verify:

- Account number.
- IFSC.
- Branch.
- Bank account details required for disbursement.

### 13.3 Blank-Dated Cheque

The blank-dated cheque is held as security.

If the borrower defaults on repayment obligations, SFPCL may insert the date and present the cheque for recovery of the outstanding amount, subject to required approvals.

### 13.4 Documents Prepared by Compliance Team

The Compliance Team prepares the full documentation set and submits it to the Sanction Committee again for approval once all documents are ready.

Documents include:

| Document | Signature / Condition |
|---|---|
| Power of Attorney | Signed by farmer and nominee; ₹500 stamp paper; notarised |
| Declaration / Tri-party Agreement | Signed by borrower and nominee |
| Share Transfer Form SH-4 | Signed by borrower and witness; applicable for physical shares |
| CDSL pledge process | Applicable where shares are in demat form |
| Term Sheet | Signed by loan applicant and nominee |
| Loan Agreement | Signed by loan applicant and witness; ₹500 stamp paper; notarised |
| Bank Verification Letter | Required where signature mismatch exists |
| Checklist | Index covering all documents |

### 13.5 Power of Attorney

The Compliance Team prepares a Power of Attorney in favour of the Company Secretary.

Purpose:

- Authorises the Company Secretary to initiate sale of shares held in SFPCL if the borrower defaults.
- Must be signed by farmer and nominee.
- Must be executed on ₹500 stamp paper.
- Must be notarised.

### 13.6 Declaration / Tri-party Agreement

The repayment process may be linked to transactions between the borrower and SFPCL’s subsidiary company.

The tri-party process is:

1. Borrower sells produce to a subsidiary company.
2. Subsidiary company pays borrower as per commercial terms.
3. While paying the borrower, subsidiary deducts amounts toward loan repayment, including:
   - Principal.
   - Interest.
   - Other loan dues.
4. Subsidiary pays the deducted amount to SFPCL.
5. SFPCL adjusts the amount toward the borrower’s outstanding loan obligation.
6. Borrower enters into a tri-party agreement with SFPCL and the relevant subsidiary company.
7. The agreement confirms transfer of amount received from the borrower toward loan repayment to the holding company, SFPCL.

### 13.7 Share Transfer Form SH-4

SH-4 is used as an additional security measure.

Required where shares are held physically.

The form must be:

- Prepared.
- Executed.
- Signed by shareholder, whether farmer or FPC.
- Signed by valid witness.

If shares are held in demat form, the online CDSL pledge process applies instead.

### 13.8 CDSL Share Pledge Process for Demat Shares

Where shares are in demat form, shares must be pledged online through CDSL.

The SOP requires:

1. Pledgor and pledgee must have Beneficiary Owner accounts with CDSL.
2. Accounts may be with the same DP or different DPs.
3. Pledgor BO ensures securities intended for pledge are in the pledgee’s approved list.
4. Pledgor BO fills Pledge Request Form in duplicate and submits it to its DP.
5. Pledgor’s DP creates pledge request in the depository system.
6. Unique Pledge Sequence Number is generated.
7. Pledge request becomes available to pledgee’s DP.
8. Pledgee submits instructions to its DP to accept or reject the pledge request.
9. If pledgee has standing instructions to accept pledges, separate acceptance instruction may not be required.
10. Pledge is created once pledgee DP accepts the pledge request.
11. Loan agreement and disbursement remain outside the depository system.
12. Pledgee only needs to inform agreement number in the pledge request form.

Any future shares issued to the borrower are to stand pledged to SFPCL.

### 13.9 Invocation of Pledge

If the loan is not repaid:

1. Pledgee instructs the DP to invoke the pledge by submitting Invocation Request Form.
2. Securities move from pledgor’s account to pledgee’s account.
3. Invocation does not require confirmation from pledgor.
4. Pledgor is informed of the movement of securities by its DP.

### 13.10 Unpledging After Repayment

After loan repayment:

1. Pledgor fills Unpledge Request Form in duplicate and submits it to its DP.
2. Pledgor’s DP creates unpledge request using the original Pledge Sequence Number.
3. Unpledge request may be for partial or full pledged quantity.
4. Pledgee’s DP accesses the request through depository system.
5. Based on URF submitted by pledgee, pledgee’s DP accepts or rejects unpledge request.
6. Securities may also be unpledged by pledgee submitting duly filled URF to its DP.
7. The latter is called Auto Unpledge and requires no instruction from pledgor.

### 13.11 Term Sheet

The Term Sheet is prepared by the Compliance Team.

It must include:

1. Borrower details.
2. Nominee details.
3. Details of shares held by borrower in SFPCL.
4. Whether loan facility is long-term or short-term.
5. Loan amount.
6. Purpose of loan.
7. Rate of interest.
8. Tenure of interest.
9. Repayment date.
10. Penalty interest.
11. Other charges / fees.
12. Security.
13. Dispute resolution.

### 13.12 Loan Agreement

The Loan Agreement is prepared after execution of the Term Sheet.

It is a legally binding document setting out all loan terms and conditions.

Requirements:

- Prepared by Compliance Team member.
- Executed on ₹500 stamp paper.
- Notarised.
- Signed by applicant and witness.

### 13.13 Bank Verification Letter

Before disbursement, the Credit Assessment Team verifies borrower signature consistency across:

- PAN card.
- Cheque.
- KYC documents.
- Other submitted documents.

If signature does not match, either of the following is required:

**Option 1: Bank Verification Letter**

- Signed and stamped by the concerned bank.
- Confirms signature on cheque belongs to account holder.
- Confirms signature is valid for transaction purposes.

**Option 2: Borrower Declaration**

- Borrower provides declaration on non-judicial stamp paper.
- Declaration affirms that the signature belongs to the loan applicant.

### 13.14 Checklist

The Checklist is an index document listing all documents in the loan file.

It is used for control, approval and final disbursement readiness.

### 13.15 Final Approvals Before Disbursement

Once all documents and the checklist are ready:

1. Compliance Team member shares file with Company Secretary.
2. Company Secretary reviews and approves.
3. File is shared with Credit Assessment Team / Credit Manager.
4. Credit Manager approves and shares file with Sanction Committee.
5. Sanction Committee provides final approval.
6. One director from Sanction Committee signs the checklist after verification, based on authority matrix.
7. File moves to Treasury Team for disbursement.
8. Senior Manager – Finance signs the checklist once actual disbursement is completed.

### 13.16 Meaning of Checklist Signatures

| Signatory | Meaning of Signature |
|---|---|
| Company Secretary | All documents required for loan disbursement have been verified and attached. |
| Credit Manager | Loan disbursement limits have been reviewed and confirmed. |
| Sanction Committee Members | Final approval for loan disbursement as per authority matrix. |
| Senior Manager – Finance | Loan has been disbursed to the applicant’s account. |

### 13.17 Term Sheet Signing Authority

In addition to checklist signatures:

- If loan amount is below ₹5 lakh, Term Sheet must be signed by CFO.
- If loan amount exceeds ₹5 lakh, Term Sheet must be signed by CFO and two directors.

## 14. Stage 5: Loan Disbursement

### 14.1 SAP Customer / Vendor Code Creation

A unique Customer ID is generated in SAP for each farmer at the time of first loan application.

Rules:

- First-time borrower: new Customer ID is created in SAP.
- Existing borrower with outstanding loan: no new Customer ID is created.
- Existing Customer ID continues for new loan.

### 14.2 Customer Code Creation Process

A unique customer code is created in SAP to record loan-related transactions systematically.

Steps:

1. Customer ID is created after Sanction Committee approval.
2. Credit Manager prepares and sends official email to Senior Manager – Finance requesting SAP customer profile creation.
3. Email includes borrower information in Excel format.

Required information:

1. Farmer’s full name.
2. Aadhaar number.
3. PAN number.
4. Address.
5. Email ID.
6. Assigned loan application number.

After creation:

- Senior Manager – Finance sends formal confirmation email to Credit Manager.
- SAP entry for initial loan payment is performed by Senior Manager – Finance based on Sanction Committee approval.

### 14.3 Disbursement Process

Disbursement steps:

1. All approvals are completed.
2. SAP code creation is initiated or in process.
3. Complete loan file is forwarded to Treasury Team.
4. Senior Manager – Finance conducts final check of approved documents and loan details.
5. Senior Manager – Finance initiates online payment through SFPCL’s RBL Bank account.
6. Transaction is forwarded to Chief Financial Controller.
7. Chief Financial Controller, as authorised signatory, provides final approval and executes bank transfer.
8. After disbursement:
   - Loan register is updated by Credit Assessment Team.
   - Disbursement advice is shared with the farmer.

At this point, loan disbursement for the application is complete.

## 15. Stage 6: Monitoring and Repayment

### 15.1 Direct Repayment by Farmer

If repayment is made directly by farmer:

- Amount is transferred to SFPCL’s account through RTGS or NEFT.
- Partial repayment is adjusted first against principal before interest recovery.
- SAP repayment entry is posted by Credit Manager on the next working day after payment is received and confirmed.

### 15.2 Repayment Through Subsidiary Company

Where subsidiary company transfers repayment to SFPCL on behalf of individual borrower:

1. Each bank statement transaction must clearly reflect:
   - Borrower name.
   - Loan application number.
2. Treasury Department verifies bank statement.
3. Treasury passes corresponding receipt entry in SAP.
4. At financial year-end, Sales Team prepares and issues interest invoices for each farmer who has availed a loan.

### 15.3 Interest Handling

If the farmer cannot pay interest up to 30 April of the next financial year:

1. Unpaid interest is added to principal at the beginning of the next financial year.
2. Interest for the new financial year is calculated on revised principal.
3. Revised principal equals:

```text
Original Principal + Outstanding Interest Carried Forward
```

4. Company informs borrower by:
   - Official email.
   - Hard copy intimation letter.

### 15.4 Full Repayment and Closure

Upon full repayment:

1. Compliance Team issues NOC to borrower.
2. Copy of SH-4 is returned.
3. Blank-dated cheque is returned.
4. Loan-related documents are archived for at least eight years.

### 15.5 Loan Tenure

The loan tenure is specified in the loan schedule from date of disbursement.

The company may extend tenure at its sole discretion.

Classification:

| Loan Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| Short-term loan | Loan for period of one year |
| Long-term loan | All other loan tenures |

### 15.6 Interest Rate

The interest rate in the Term Sheet is a floating rate of interest.

It changes based on bank rates.

Revised interest rates are communicated to borrowers by:

- SMS.
- Email.

### 15.7 Repayment Monitoring

Monitoring is performed through SAP.

Process:

- Accounts Team monitors repayments quarterly.
- Credit Manager classifies loans by DPD buckets.
- Quarterly MIS is presented to CFO.
- Credit Manager sends reminders by SMS / phone when loan remains outstanding beyond one year at the end of each quarter.

DPD buckets in the SOP:

1. 1 year to 2 years.
2. 2 years to 3 years.
3. More than 3 years.

### 15.8 Interest Invoices and Accrual Entries

Credit Manager is responsible for:

- Generating yearly interest invoices.
- Posting accrual entries in SAP monthly.

## 16. Default Handling

### 16.1 Initial Missed Scheduled Principal Repayment

If borrower is unable to repay principal amount for one scheduled repayment:

- SFPCL provides further tenure of three months.
- The three-month grace period runs from the due date of scheduled repayment.

### 16.2 Post-Grace Review

If complete principal repayment is not made within the further three months:

- Credit Assessment Team checks reason for non-payment.
- Team analyses whether non-payment is intentional or non-intentional.

### 16.3 Non-Intentional Non-Payment

If non-payment is non-intentional:

- Company provides extension for one further year.
- Credit Manager prepares an extension note.
- Extension note is kept documented in the loan file.

### 16.4 Still Unpaid After One-Year Extension

If borrower is unable to repay principal even after one-year extension:

1. Loan is considered for scrutiny.
2. Loan is treated as not recoverable.
3. Matter goes back to Credit Assessment Team for further scrutiny.
4. Credit Assessment Team checks reason for non-payment again.
5. Team analyses whether non-payment is intentional or non-intentional.
6. Credit Assessment Team prepares a Note for Non-Payment.
7. Note is shared with Sanction Committee for further action approval.

### 16.5 Sanction Committee Decision on Recovery Action

Based on:

- Note for Non-Payment.
- Discussion with Credit Assessment Team.

The Sanction Committee decides whether to:

- Initiate sale of shares.
- Execute / present undated cheque taken as security.
- Take other recovery action.

Use of SH-4 or undated cheque must not be informal and must be properly approved.

## 28. Executive Summary for Client

SFPCL’s Member Credit SOP defines a robust but control-heavy lending process for loans to members. The process begins with a borrower application, moves through eligibility checks and appraisal, proceeds to Sanction Committee approval, then requires extensive documentation, stamping, SAP setup and bank disbursement before entering repayment monitoring and closure.

The core of the process is governance: only members may borrow; loan limits depend on shareholding and land-based scale of finance; approvals vary by amount; documentation must be complete before disbursement; and defaults must move through a staged recovery route before security is invoked.

The biggest implementation risks are not merely operational. They include unresolved policy issues, such as the 30% vs 10% loan-limit contradiction, undefined interest and penal charge rules, unclear NACH / guarantor / bureau-check requirements, and annexure numbering conflicts. These must be resolved before automation or final rollout.

A well-designed system or process transformation should preserve the SOP’s controls while reducing manual dependency on Excel registers, email approvals, physical checklist tracking and fragmented SAP / bank evidence. The ideal target state is a single controlled workflow where each loan has a traceable application, appraisal, approval, document, disbursement, repayment, monitoring, recovery and closure record.
